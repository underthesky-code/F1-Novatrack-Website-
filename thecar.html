Design Philosophy

HOW THE SUPERNOVA WAS BORN

The SuperNova brings together the best-performing features from eight distinct prototypes, refined through iterative CFD simulation. Its parabolique nose triggers laminar airflow from the very front. A manta-ray inspired curved underbody accelerates air beneath the car, lowering the centre of gravity and improving stability. At the rear, a Kammtail-inspired tail minimises wake while a loop-style rear wing (Horizon) reduces tip vortices — the same principle used on winglets in commercial aviation.

Wing Development

AERO COMPONENTS

All front wings were designed on a ramp principle to redirect airflow over the wheels. The rear wing goal was minimising drag on a straight-line circuit — leading to the loop-style Horizon design.

Ion — Front Wing

Short ramp, straight attachment point. High stability, easy to manufacture.

Forge — Front Wing

Longer ramp with slower curve. Manta-ray-inspired sweep using Fusion 360 loft tool.

Helix — Front Wing

Widening ramp to reduce drag. Curved to merge with nose cone.

Prism — Front Wing

Low, curved profile. Best at shielding front wheels from turbulent airflow. Used on final design.

Zenith — Rear Wing

Simple curved spoiler. Curved edges reduce separation. Inspired by upswept aircraft winglets.

Horizon — Rear Wing ✦

Loop-style rear wing eliminates tip vortices. Reduces drag on straight circuit. Used on final design.
